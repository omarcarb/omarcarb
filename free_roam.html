Free Roam

An app with loads of options for those with limited mobility

Overview

Project Summary

Free Roam is a mobile app project. It's designed with the sole purpose of providing easy access to para-transit services and information about accessibility service at any destination. The project time was between July 2022 and August 2022.

Problem That Needs To Be Solved

The elderly and people with mobility disadvantages have trouble finding places to travel to that accomedate their every need. Even worse, they have no proper way of getting there since most apps heavily focus on able bodied people. The problem is the lack of accessibiltity to such services that bring para-transit service and information about a locations accessibility service in the palm of their hand.

The Solution

Create an app that allows for users to select any destination and order any means of transportation to get there as well as preview a locations accessibility services for easier planning.

Process to Product

I ran into the first challenge when designing this app. I am an able bodied person and I have no clue what the experience of a person with limited mobility is like. As such, I had to reach out to friends and family who have experienced or are currently expereincing issues with their mobility.
Here is the process I went though.

Process

Research

Engage with people who currently have issues with mobility and how they travel.

Ideation

Come up with as many ideas as possible and pick the best one from the bunch

Testing

Conduct a series of test with a wide variety of background and disabilities to get as much feedback.

Design

Finalize the process by coming up with the final look and feel of the product

Ideation

To make the process of ordering para transport as simple and hassle free as possible, I thought of a simple 5 page ordering process allowing for quick and easy access to a variety of travel options.

Laying the Foundation

It has been proven that dark mode is much easier on the eyes and allows for greater accessibility than using a light background. As such, I came to the decision to make the entire app designed soley in a dark mode aesthetic.

What I have Learned

  • Dark mode is probably the best choice for easier accessibility since it allows those with vision problems to better see the content.

  • Users prefer the results to be more personal as many have complained that no relevant information was being shown. Maybe in a future iteration, I could implenent more personalized search results.

  • Streamlining processes such as ordering a ride should be priority number 1, especially when it involves accessibility. The more streamlined the proccess, the higher the conversion rate.

  • Due to the unrelenting amount of user research I had to conduct, it gave me better insight on how to design around ones disabilities, which I will definitely put to use in future projects.
