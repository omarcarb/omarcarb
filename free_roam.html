What's the issue?

In the digital age, people with limited mobility, like seniors and those with disabilities, still struggle to find accessible transportation and destination info, limiting their community mobility.

Market Research

The Data

In 2017, 13.9% of people with disabilities drove / traveled regularly (down from 17.5% in 2009), a concerning pattern that restricts their mobility, access to essential services, and employment opportunities due to the constraints posed by their disabilities, underscoring the pressing need for enhanced para transit services and improved accessibility.

The Problem

People with disabilities tend to travel less due to a lack of avaliable resources that make traveling not only easier but safer.

Competitive Analysis

The Competition

As part of my research, I study and took notes on 3 of the most popular map applications and website and noted what accessibility features they offered. These three apps were Google Maps, Apple Maps, and Bing Maps

The Good

  • All apps offer different methods of transportation to reach a destination
  • All apps show a locations accessibility options

The Bad

  • None of the apps offer para-transit or alternative modes of transportation
  • There are no options to choose locations based on accessibility needs
  • Google Maps and Apple Maps require a bit of navigating to see information on a locations accessibility options

Based On Initial Research

The data reveals a notable decline in travel among individuals with disabilities, highlighting their restricted access to essential services and employment opportunities due to inadequate transportation options. The survey findings echo this, emphasizing the prevalent issue of limited accessibility for those with disabilities.

A potential solution facilitating access to para-transit services and comprehensive information on accessibility features could significantly alleviate these challenges, offering greater independence and improved access to necessary services for individuals with disabilities.
