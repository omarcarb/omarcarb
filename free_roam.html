Free Roam

An app with loads of options for those with limited mobility

Overview

Project Summary

Free Roam is an app with the sole purpose of providing easy access to para-transit service and information about accessibility service at any destination. The project took place between June and July of 2023

Problem That Needs To Be Solved

Finding a place that can provide proper accomodations for a disabled person's needs is difficult, even in this age of easy access to information. The problems that need to be addressed are:

1

Not enough services providing alternative modes of transport.

2

Locations not providing information about their accomodation services.

3

As a result, most elderly and people with disabilities have trouble traveling around town.

The Solution

Design an app that helps the elderly and people with mobility issues to help them move around. The app must:

1

Provide alternative modes of transport for everyone

2

List out all information of a location's accomodation services.

3

The app must be accessible to people of all physical abilities and ages.

My Role

Wireframing, Prototyping, Ideating, Research

Process to Product

I ran into the first challenge when designing this app. I am an able bodied person and I have no clue what the experience of a person with limited mobility is like. As such, I had to reach out to friends and family who have experienced or are currently expereincing issues with their mobility.


Here is the process I went though:

Research

Engage with people who currently have issues with mobility and how they travel.

Ideation

Come up with as many ideas as possible and pick the best one from the bunch

Testing

Conduct a series of test with a wide variety of background and disabilities to get as much feedback.

Design

Finalize the process by coming up with the final look and feel of the product

Ideation

To make the process of ordering para-transit as simple and hassle free as possible, I thought of a simple 5 page ordering process allowing for quick and easy access to a variety of travel options.

Testing

Results

I measured success based on the time on a task the user spent. Findings for the study can be summarized as such:

1

Users were frustrated as to why the home screen wasn't the map screen

2

Button layouts were confusing.

3

Information was not shared properly.

4

Visually impaired individuals had a tough time navigating.

What to Do

I needed to make an app that had as little hurdles to the end goal as possible. I got rid of the home screen entirely as it was not needed, and moved a few buttons around to make them easier to spot.

Design

Laying the Foundation

It has been proven that dark mode is much easier on the eyes and allows for greater accessibility than using a light background. As such, I came to the decision to make the entire app designed soley in a dark mode aesthetic.

Final Product

Results and Takeaways

Participants have overall expressed satisfaction over the recent changes made to the app, as they have found it significantly easier to use and better to navigate. A big plus was that I have made it incredibly accessible to people with vision impairments, which accounts for a large chunk of the elderly population.

Some things that I took away from completing this project are:

1

Dark mode is probably the best choice for easier accessibility. It allows those with vision problems to better see the content.

2

Users prefer the results to be more personal. Many have complained that no relevant information was being shown. Maybe in a future iteration, I could implenent more personalized search results.

3

Streamlining processes, such as ordering a ride, should be priority number 1. The more streamlined the proccess, the higher the conversion rate.

In Conclusion

An app like this is absolutley neccessary, especially for people who struggle to get around as much as others. In all the interviews I have conducted, many have expressed desire for an application that would allow them to travel their hometown a lot easier. This could potentially lead to more accessible travel in the future.
