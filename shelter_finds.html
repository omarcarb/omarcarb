Market Research

What The Research Shows

Renters are becoming more selective, prioritizing amenities like gyms, pet areas, and broadband internet. The Zillow Consumer Housing Trends Report shows a rise in demand for fitness gyms from 15% in 2022 to 24% in 2023, with 48% considering broadband essential. Technavio's research predicts the US tiny homes market will exceed $5 billion by 2024, emphasizing the importance of modern amenities and flexible living options in rental decisions.

What The Research Concludes

Renters today demand properties that cater to their specific preferences and lifestyle needs. This growing desire for personalized accommodation is driving the development of sophisticated matching methods. Innovations in rental platforms are using advanced algorithms and user data to facilitate tailored rental experiences, leading to higher satisfaction and retention rates.

Competitive Analysis

The first step in my design process was to analyze how the competition approached the problem. I selected two competitors as my main sources of inspiration.

Rent.com
Zillow

What These Apps Do Right:

Both offer services for both seeking renters and sellers

Rent.com allows for filtering for ammenities

Zillow includes a broader range of property types

What These Apps Do Wrong:

There is limited to no personalized searches

Zillows property selection is very limited

Rent.com only looks for apartments and nothing beyond that

Conclusion

In an age where properties like container homes and tiny houses exist, many rental sites offer a limited variety of property selections. While Zillow provides a slightly broader range of property types, it still excludes many alternative options.

The absence of specific filters makes each search feel the same, creating a monotonous user experience. Users often find it difficult to refine their search criteria, which leads to a limited selection and difficulty in finding properties that match their unique needs and preferences on these websites

Amenities and other perks are not the central focus for all properties; instead, there is a greater emphasis on aesthetics rather than the practical features and benefits offered.

Prototype Validation

It was time to put the designs and flows to the test. I assembled a small group of around five individuals to assist in testing the website and provide feedback on its functionality and visual appeal.

Each participant engaged in a straightforward 20 to 30-minute exercise, tasked with completing a single o bjective. Conducted entirely remotely and without moderation, the exercise involved scheduling a tour while browsing for a house of their preference. This test was specifically crafted to identify any potential challenges users might encounter in finding their ideal property.

Before

In this iteration, filters were not seperated and were all lumped together. At the time, I thought it would be beneficial since users would be able to select a variety of filters, but it caused frustrations as users complained about not finding the right filter.

After

After the revision, all filters had seperate categories assigned to them, with heading and proper spacing to distingush them from each other. In addition, I changed the input to some of the options from checkboxes to buttons and sliders.

Study Results

2 out of 5 users mentioned issues with filtering the rental selection. They said that the filter options were overwhelming and that they were confused as to what to pick since there was no difference between the items on screen.

New and Improved

To address the initial issue, I restructured the filters by categorizing them separately. This involved introducing bold heading text for each category and clearly separating them from one another. Additionally, I adjusted certain inputs to clarify their corresponding categories. Finally, I ensured that each category maintained its uniqueness while adhering to a consistent design language.

Final Thoughts and Future Plans

Through this project, I've come to understand that users value information without it feeling overwhelming. Additionally, I've learned that simplicity often leads to the most effective design solutions. Moving forward, I plan to refine the design, implementing changes to accommodate screen readers and other accessibility tools. This will enhance the site's usability for all users, particularly those with disabilities, ensuring a more inclusive experience.
