Market Research

What The Research Shows

Renters are increasingly selective, prioritizing amenities like gyms, pet areas, and broadband internet. The percentage seeking a fitness gym rose from 15% in 2022 to 24% in 2023, with 48% considering broadband essential, according to the Zillow Consumer Housing Trends Report. Technavio's research indicates the tiny homes market may grow by $3.57 billion from 2021 to 2026, driven by affordability and sustainability, with North America leading this growth. The US tiny home market is projected to exceed $5 billion by 2024. These trends emphasize the importance of modern amenities and flexible living options in rental decisions and satisfaction

What The Research Concludes

Renters today are demanding rental properties that cater to their specific preferences and lifestyle needs. This increasing desire for personalized accommodation is fueling growth in the rental market, prompting the development of more sophisticated matching methods. Innovations in rental applications and platforms are leveraging advanced algorithms and user data to facilitate this personalized matching process. This shift towards tailored rental experiences underscores the importance of understanding and catering to the diverse needs of today's renters, ultimately leading to higher satisfaction and retention rates.

Competitive Analysis

The first step in my design process was to look at how the competition approached the problem. I chose 2 competitors as my main inspiration

Rent.com
Zillow

What These Websites Do Right:

Google Maps offers accessibility information about a specific location

Uber allows for specific pick up instructions

myRTCpara offers paratransit services in a dedicated app

What These Websites Do Wrong:

Google Maps doesn’t offer transport alternatives

Uber doesn’t offer paratransit services

myRTCpara is local and their services are not available everywhere

Conclusion

Competitors offer some accessibility features but don’t offer much in the way of delivering a service that allows individuals with disabilities to move around their city.

Competitors that do offer a service for people with disabilities, they are all local services with no nationwide support. If a user were to travel abroad, they would lose that service.

Accommodations are a last consideration for most of the competitors. Those that do offer accommodations are services that specialize in those field but are local.
