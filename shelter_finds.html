Shelter Finds

Your one stop for all of your residency needs.

Overview

Project Summary

Shelter Finds is a website for people looking to rent property. What makes Shelter Finds unique is the extensive list of rental properties available. From the common apartments to small container homes.

Problem That Needs To Be Solved

Users want to find a property that fits their specific needs and lifestyle. Many websites offer such a service, but their selection is limited. Those that do offer a wide selection suffer from having too much available to the user. This causes stress among rental seekers and wastes valuable time.

The Solution

Create a website that offers a ton of filter options and make it easy to navigate.

My Role

Wireframing, Prototyping, Ideating, Research

Process to Product

Most rental websites follow a simple 3 step formula for getting the property the user wants.

1. Search for properties in the immediate area.

2. List qualities people in the area generally look for.

3. Narrow the search results with filters (such as allowing pets in the rental property).

To deliver what the users would want, I had to come up with a way for users navigate the website. This also needs to accomplished while providing a wide variety of properties.

To accomplish this goal, I followed this methodology:

Research

Find out how the competition solves this problem, if they have solved it at all.

Ideation

Come up with as many ideas as possible and pick the best one from the bunch

Testing

See if the ideas work in practice and keep testing until it works

Design

Finalize the process by coming up with the final look and feel of the product

Ideation

To allow for the display of tons of properties, I came up with several iterations of the website layout. I also wanted the images to be as prominent as possible for faster decision making. A lot of the early designs are inspired by websites such as Rent.com and Zillow. I decided to go with this simplistic layout with a heavy emphasis on visuals.

Design

Laying the Foundation

Other rental websites go for a minimalistic aesthetic to convey a lot of information. While this is effective, its not very distinct from other website. As such, I went with a more Aurora Gradient aesthetic with soft gradients and vibrant colors. This helps the website stand out from others, while also giving visual feedback to the user.

Testing and Layout

Wireframes and Low Fidelity Prototypes

Heirarchy website structure seems like the best way to navigate their user to thier destination. It allows for infinite expansion for individual properties and plenty more room for other sections if they are needed.

What I have Learned

  • People overwhelmingly seem to prefer properties close to family than having a unique home.

  • Gradient designs are really liked by many people but do present a challenge to those that are slightly visually impaired.

  • Having a lot of filters can be overwhelming to some users. Many prefered having the filters hidden over having them all visible while scrolling.
