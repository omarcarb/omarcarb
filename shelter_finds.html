Shelter Finds

Your one stop for all of your residency needs.

Overview

Project Summary

"Shelter Finds" is a website dedicated to locating rental properties that cater to a range of preferences, encompassing not only individuals with aspirations of homeownership but also those seeking accommodations aligned with unique tastes. The project duration spanned from November to December 2022.

Problem That Needs To Be Solved

Unearthing the ideal property for purchase or rental presents hurdles, including sifting through listings, deciphering amenities, and matching preferences with options. To fix this, it is the responsibility of the website to:

1

Offer locations near or around the desire location of the user

2

List qualities people in the area generally look for.

The Solution

Design an app tailored to the needs of rental and property seekersThe app must:

1

Search for properties in the immediate area or properties in other locations.

2

List qualities people in the area generally look for.

3

Narrow the search results with filters (such as allowing pets in the rental property).

My Role

Wireframing, Prototyping, Ideating, Research

Process to Product

Most rental websites follow a simple 3 step formula for getting the property the user wants.

1

Search for properties in the immediate area or properties in other locations.

2

List qualities people in the area generally look for.

3

Narrow the search results with filters (such as allowing pets in the rental property).

In pursuit of user-centric design, devising a seamless website navigation strategy became imperative. This endeavor was further enriched by the challenge of ensuring a diverse array of properties is easily accessible to users.

To accomplish this goal, I followed this methodology:

Research

Find out how the competition solves this problem, if they have solved it at all.

Ideation

Come up with as many ideas as possible and pick the best one from the bunch

Testing

See if the ideas work in practice and keep testing until it works

Design

Finalize the process by coming up with the final look and feel of the product

Ideation

To facilitate the presentation of an extensive range of properties, I embarked on crafting multiple iterations of the website layout. Emphasizing the efficiency of decision-making, I prioritized prominent imagery. Many of the initial design concepts drew inspiration from platforms like Rent.com and Zillow. The final choice gravitated towards a clean, simplified layout that places a significant emphasis on visual elements.

Testing

Results

I measured success based on the time on a task the user spent. Findings for the study can be summarized as such:

1

Many users were confused when it came down to the navigation of the website.

2

Button layouts were confusing.

3

Users wondered why there was only the ability to choose rental units, such as apartments or house, but not alternative rentals, such as container homes.

4

Some users pointed out that the a lot of the information provided by the websites was unnecessary.

What to Do

Users showed a preference for a cleaner screen with less information rather than an overloaded one. With this insight, the strategic direction was to offer users a wide array of options without overwhelming them visually. Subsequently, I revisited the design and opted for a more minimalist approach.

Design

Laying the Foundation

Other rental websites go for a minimalistic aesthetic to convey a lot of information. While this is effective, its not very distinct from other website. As such, I went with a more Aurora Gradient aesthetic with soft gradients and vibrant colors. This helps the website stand out from others, while also giving visual feedback to the user.

Results and Takeaways

Participants impressions on the website improved by a substantial margin. Many calling the website easy to use and colorful to look at. However, all participants have expressed doubts about using the website for their personal searches, which is understandable. Despite this I have learned plenty from this project.

Some notable lessons include:

1

Presenting to many choice to people can lead to low retention rate. As such, Its better to show few options

2

Gradient designs are really liked by many people but do present a challenge to those that are slightly visually impaired.

3

Having a lot of filters can be overwhelming to some users. Many prefered having the filters hidden over having them all visible while scrolling.

In Conclusion

Although not my finest work to date, I regard this project as an important stepping stone in my UI/UX journey. Even in moments of setback, there were valuable lessons to be learned from designing something unique.
